Are your taxes fucked?

Your taxes might be fucked if...

  • You haven't filed a tax return in years and you're just hoping the IRS forgets about you
  • You got a letter from the IRS and put it in a drawer instead of opening it
  • You owe the IRS more than you can pay and have no idea what your options are
  • Your wages are being garnished and you're living paycheck to paycheck
  • The IRS put a lien on your property and you found out from your bank
  • You're a 1099 contractor who hasn't been paying quarterly estimates
  • You got audited and you have no receipts, records, or explanation
  • You owe payroll taxes from your business and the IRS is coming after you personally
  • You're paying penalties and interest that are bigger than your original tax debt
  • You filed your own taxes and now you're getting notices you don't understand
  • You used TurboTax and it turns out you missed thousands in deductions
  • You're self-employed and you have no idea what you actually owe
  • You sold crypto and didn't report it because you thought the IRS wouldn't find out
  • You have foreign bank accounts and you've never heard of an FBAR
  • Your bookkeeping is a shoebox full of receipts from three years ago
  • You claimed your dog as a dependent (seriously, don't do this)
  • You're married filing separately when you should be filing jointly, costing you thousands
  • You haven't paid estimated taxes all year and April is coming fast
  • Your business owes state sales tax and you've been ignoring the notices
  • You set up an LLC but you're still paying taxes like a sole proprietor
  • You're paying an accountant who files extensions every year and never calls you back
  • You have unfiled returns from a business that closed years ago
  • The IRS rejected your Offer in Compromise and you don't know why
  • You're in "Currently Not Collectible" status but you have no plan for when that expires
  • You owe taxes from a divorce settlement and your ex isn't helping
  • You withdrew from your 401(k) early and didn't set aside money for taxes
  • You got unemployment benefits and forgot that's taxable income
  • You work gig economy jobs and you're treating gross income as net income
  • You haven't filed because you can't pay, not realizing that makes it worse
  • You're getting Social Security and you didn't know it could be taxable
  • You paid someone cash to "take care of your taxes" and now you're not sure if they actually filed
  • You think you can just ignore the IRS and eventually they'll go away (they won't)
